<h1>Arcanic Equilibrium</h1>
<p>Arcanic Equilibrium (or Æ/AE) is a datapack inspired by the CTM-MMO-RPG server Monumenta. It adds (or at least, will eventually add):</p>

<ul>
  <li>A deadly dimension</li>
  <li>A Doctor Who TARDIS-clone to explore that dimension with</li>
  <li>A mysterious runic language for players to decipher and use like Ars Magicka / Mana and Artifice / Ars Nouveau's spellmaking system</li>
  <li>6 classes and various attached systems</li>
  <li>Various enhanced mobs and custom bosses to fight with said classes</li>
  <li>A story about protecting the Arcanic Equilibrium</li>
  <li>and maybe more. Who knows.</li>
</ul>

<p>I really hope I get the time to make all of this. If I can even make at least half of it, I'll be happy. I am a completely amateur datapack developer
  but I'm doing my best to learn what I can! Here are those things on the list, more in-depth</p>

<h2>A deadly dimension - the Ravaged Realm (RR)</h2>
<p>A land riddled with bizarre anomalies, ruins, and deadly magical creatures, everywhere. Corrupted by eldritch forces, this once-vibrant world has fallen to ruin.
Learn the Ravaged Realm's story, explore the vast world it presents, scavenge for useful resources, and beware, you're not the only one living in this world.</p>
<p>(To figure out how to get to the RR, see the TARDIS-clone section)</p>

<p>As you expose yourself to this deeply corrupted realm longer, you get corrupted further and your 'scent' becomes stronger; <b>You have a time limit</b>.
As it reaches certain thresholds, you may receive certain debuffs. Good luck fighting *them* once the time limit's over.</p>

<h2>A TARDIS-clone - the RIFT</h2>
<p>Your way in and out of the RR, and other dimensions. Going inside the RIFT leads to a cleverly-designed control room, somewhat worn thanks to the cruel effects of time.
The effects of the RR's Corruption will not reach you here, and will instead die down over time.</p>

<p><b>Each input does something different.</b> I specifically wanted to design a
  TARDIS that emulated the same feeling as the Doctor circling about his own console, strategically
whacking switches with hammers, pulling zigzag plotters, flicking switches, etc. None of the TARDIS-related content out there that I had seen
was capable of providing the same feeling to me; in most of them, it was as simple as just punching in numbers into coordinates, selecting a dimension,
maybe doing a few quick-time-events, and voila, you've moved a few thousand blocks in an instant. Journeys in the RIFT are meant to feel much more involved, instead of simply being used as
a glorified /teleport.</p>

<p><b>If you don't want to get bogged down by the complexity of it, that's fine.</b> I completely understand. There are
simple methods you can learn based on a provided manual, such as calculating and setting the angle to your destination.</p>

<p>Some mechanics include:</p>
<ul>
  <li>The aforementioned Angle Encoder</li>
  <li>Creating mini wormholes via a Wormhole Interpolator</li>
  <li>The Spatial Highway that boosts speed whenever you're at the top height of a dimension</li>
  <li>A Dimensional Shifter</li>
  <li>Engine Strain that impacts various aspects of flight</li>
  <li>Vulnerabilities like certain mobs being able to smell the RIFT, enter it, and take the fight to you inside even if you're trying to escape</li>
  <li>Various systems such as a radar for the outside, fancy lights, and a system to reallocate energy to make some systems perform better at the cost of other systems' performances/li>
</ul>
<p>Mastery is not necessary but very much encouraged and rewarded with significantly more speed than just using angles. Just like the TARDIS and the combat in this datapack,
this will be designed for 2-4 pilots. Solo is still  possible, but may give you more headaches. Good luck.</p>


<h2>A magic runic language - 'Linnic'</h2>
<p>Heavily inspired by the mod Mana and Artifice in particular, this language allows players to write sentences that cast spells. You may write spells in the air using a Runeweaver,
and you are limited to a certain number of characters when you begin. Your Runeweaver will be placed on cooldown based on the spell's effect and how many characters it took to write it.
The mechanics of this language and how different symbols, words, or meanings are made, are meant to be discovered through good ol' trial and error in liaison with knowledge gained from exploring the RR.</p>



<h2>6 classes and various attached systems</h2>
<p>There was once a great magical kingdom in a time before the lands were corrupted. They were lead by 6 great people:</p>

<ul>
  <li>Arphos, the Stargazer</li>
  <li>Reima, the Warrior</li>
  <li>Kuto, the Alchemist</li>
  <li>Lipha, the Chronomancer</li>
  <li>Artol, the Cleric</li>
  <li>Odril, the Hexmaker</li>
</ul>

<p>Together, they made up the Elite. You'll learn more about them as you go about following the same paths they took to become as powerful as they were. Will you learn the wise wisdom Arphos found
in the beauty of the stars above, or will you learn the devastating debuffing potential of Odril's hexmaking? Will you ruthlessly destroy all the undead monstrosities
you encounter, like Cleric Artol? Will you see the chaos of the world around you for what it is and embrace it, like Alchemist Kuto? Will you become a master of the blade
like Warrior Reima, or will you destroy your enemies in an instant like Chronomancer Lipha?</p>

<p>Like in Monumenta, <b>you are NOT locked to one class!</b> While you can only have one class's skills at any one time, you can unlock all of them,
choose between them and pick their skills without cost. You are tasked with following ALL of the Elite's journeys, after all. They all have different playstyles,
so try them all out and pick whatever you fancy. Furthermore, just because you're a Cleric doesn't mean all you can do is heal, nor does being a Hexmaker mean all you can do is hex.
Try them all out! :)</p>

<p>Casting skills involves holding a caster item and using any of Q, F, Shift, Right-click, or a combination of them. Each class has a central gimmick upon which their whole playstyle hinges around:</p>

<ul>
  <li>Stargazers have Constelled Inspiration, where over time they receive small buffs that build over time and eventually create Constellations to perform strong attacks.</li>
  <li>Warriors have Stances, where they may switch between two or more different versions of their skills to achieve certain effects, e.g. Attack stance may provide stronger attacks but a weaker parry than the Defensive stance.</li>
  <li>Alchemists have Elemental Attunement, where they tap into the randomness of elements to summon different constructs or do different attacks based on the current element.</li>
  <li>Chronomancers don't need a gimmick other than the theme of time manipulation, which is already significant enough on its own. They can slow down or entirely freeze enemies, 'reload' enemy locations to gain better positioning, or make a Dr Strange-esque sacrifice to tank damage and reset themselves to a point before taking said damage.</li>
  <li>Clerics have a Font of Mana, where they summon a Font to establish their dominion of control over life and death - will you save the lives of your allies within it or destroy the enemies who dare threaten them in the first place? </li>
  <li>Hexmakers have ____ (this is the least worked on class right now, however the general gist is that it has a stealthy, rogue-ish vibe, coupled with the ability to debuff and sabotage foes from afar or up-close)</li>
</ul>

<p>Unlocking each class also permanently gives you access to another mechanic thematically tied to the Elite member who you're following. Learn Talismithing, Armorsmithing, Brewing, Chronorestoration, Cooking, and Runic Imbuement, as you get stronger skills.</p>
